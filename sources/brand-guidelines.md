## Brand Voice

### Core Attributes
- **Approachable** — We speak like a knowledgeable colleague, not a corporate entity
- **Confident** — We are direct and assured without being arrogant
- **Helpful** — Every interaction should leave the customer better informed
- **Human** — We acknowledge mistakes, show empathy, and avoid robotic language

### Voice Principles
1. Use "we" and "our" — the brand speaks as a team, not an institution
2. Prefer active voice — "We'll fix this" not "This will be resolved"
3. Be specific — "by Friday" not "shortly", "3 working days" not "a few days"
4. Match formality to context — internal comms can be casual; external should be warm but professional

## Writing Standards

### Capitalisation
- Sentence case for headings and subheadings
- Title case only for proper nouns and product names
- Never use ALL CAPS outside of acronyms

### Numbers and Dates
- Spell out one to nine, use numerals for 10+
- Dates: "14 March 2026" (no ordinals, no American format)
- Times: "2:30 pm" (lowercase, space before am/pm)
- Currency: "£120" (no decimal for round amounts), "£120.50" (two decimal places when needed)

### Punctuation
- Oxford comma: yes, always
- Em dashes with spaces: " — " not "—"
- Single quotes for emphasis or unusual usage, double quotes for direct speech
- Exclamation marks: one per document maximum, and only when genuinely enthusiastic

## Tone Calibration

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Marketing copy | Enthusiastic, benefit-led | "Ship faster with workflows that just work" |
| Support response | Empathetic, solution-focused | "I understand the frustration — let's get this sorted" |
| Documentation | Clear, instructional | "Select the workflow tab, then click Import" |
| Error messages | Honest, helpful | "Something went wrong saving your changes. Your work is safe — try again in a moment" |
| Release notes | Concise, factual | "Fixed an issue where exported files could lose formatting" |
